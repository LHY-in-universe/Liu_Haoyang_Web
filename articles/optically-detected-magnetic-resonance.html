Optically Detected Magnetic Resonance (ODMR) is used to detect the translational and rotational motion of diamond particles. A 594-nm laser is used for the excitation of NV centers, which should always open. The sample is set at a home built confocal microscope. 2 Avalanche Photodiode (APD) are used to detect photons, one for translation and the other for rotation. Two magnetic fields are used for rotational part.

Rotational tracking

The NV centers in a diamond crystal have four inequivalent crystallographic directions. In a weak external magnetic field $B$ (<100 Gauss), the angle $\theta$ between the NV axis and the magnetic field determines the frequencies $f_{\pm}\approx D\pm \gamma_e B \cos \theta$ of the transitions between the $ğ‘š_s=0$ spin state and the $m_s=\pm 1$ states.

The frequency of the microwave is swept through $ğ‘“_1, ğ‘“_2 , .., $ and $ğ‘“_n$ with a period of $ğ‘¡= 7 ms$. The rotation of the diamond is unambiguously determined by fitting the two ODMR spectra:
